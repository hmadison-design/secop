How to use Second Opinion

What this app is. Second Opinion was developed by instructional designer and pilot Harvey Madison, co-host of On Second Thought, to assist pilots in making more objective, data-informed decisions about their fitness to fly at any given moment.

The underlying tasks draw on established brain-science paradigms: a simple reaction-time task related to the Psychomotor Vigilance Task used in fatigue research; a color–word interference (Stroop) task for executive control; and a 2-back working-memory task widely used in cognitive neuroscience.

The app is designed to be one tool among many in your IM SAFE and ADM workflow. These tools support your decision-making; they do not replace your CFI, the regulations, or your own judgment. All go/no-go decisions remain entirely the responsibility of each pilot—just as they always have.

When to use it. Use Second Opinion before a flight when you want a quick, private check of your cognitive sharpness, alongside IM SAFE, weather, and the rest of your preflight workflow.

Step 1: Build a baseline. On days when you feel well-rested, well-fed, not stressed, and absolutely comfortable flying, run the Cognition & Acuity Assessment a few times (ideally 3–5 sessions). Add only those “good-day” runs to your baseline. Future runs are compared to that personal baseline instead of an abstract population norm.

The three pieces.

  • Reaction: tap as quickly as you can when the circle turns green. Slower median reaction times and more lapses suggest reduced alertness.
  • Color–word (Stroop): decide whether the ink color matches the word. Conflicting items require you to suppress the automatic reading response—this taps executive control.
  • 2-back: watch short groups of digits and decide if the third matches the digit two positions back. This stresses online updating and working memory. The summary statistic (d′) is essentially a signal-to-noise measure; higher is better separation between “same” and “not same”.

Interpreting results. The app focuses on how far you are from your own baseline, not anyone else’s.

  • “Near baseline” looks like a typical good day—assuming your device is behaving.
  • “Mildly” or “moderately” below baseline suggests you’re softer than usual; consider adding margin or delaying until after rest, food, or reduced stress.
  • “Well below baseline” is an “On Second Thought…” moment: either something is wrong with the device, or you are not bringing your usual cognitive resources to the flight.

Limitations. A good score does not guarantee fitness to fly; a soft score does not prove unfitness. Treat this as a conservative nudge, not a verdict.

About On Second Thought

On Second Thought is a safety-focused series for general aviation pilots who want more than airplane porn and hangar talk. Each episode takes a single real-world accident and unpacks the human factors behind it—attention, bias, pressure, habit patterns, and all the tiny choices that add up to a big outcome.

The show is co-hosted by instructional designer and GA pilot Harvey Madison and psychotherapist / long-time aviation safety advocate Jolie Lucas. Together they blend solid research, story-driven case studies, and practical “what would you actually do in this cockpit?” discussions aimed squarely at line pilots— not just test pilots or airline crews.

Second Opinion is part of that ecosystem: a quick, private way to pause, think twice, and give yourself a reality check before you go fly on purpose.

About the Cognition & Acuity Assessment

This assessment is an experimental, on-device tool inspired by IM SAFE and human-factors research. It samples three domains:

  • Reaction: how quickly you respond to a simple “go” cue.
  • Color–Word (Stroop): how well you resist interference between words and colors.
  • 2-back: a small working-memory task, similar to what’s used in many lab studies.

The goal isn’t to diagnose anything. It’s to give you a private, repeatable sense of whether today feels like one of your “good” days, a “soft” day, or a “not today” day—before you add the workload of a flight.

All data stays on this device. It’s a supplement to IM SAFE and good ADM, not a replacement.
